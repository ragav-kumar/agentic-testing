What Causes Lyme Disease?

Lyme disease is caused by the bacterium Borrelia burgdorferi, transmitted through the bite of infected black-legged (deer) ticks. It is endemic across the northeastern and upper midwestern United States, as well as parts of Europe and Asia.

⚠️

Early vs. Late Symptoms

Early (days–weeks): Bull's-eye rash (erythema migrans), fever, fatigue, headache, joint pain.

Late (weeks–months): Arthritis, neurological issues, heart problems, and chronic fatigue — often requiring prolonged treatment.

🐾

Not Just Humans

Dogs, horses, cattle, and other animals are also susceptible to Lyme disease. LymeAlert works on ticks removed from any host — protecting your whole family, including four-legged members.

🕐

Time Is Everything

Current blood tests can't detect Lyme disease for 4–6 weeks after infection. LymeAlert tests the tick itself — giving you actionable information before the bacteria even enter the bloodstream.

Contact your doctor or an urgent care provider as soon as possible. When Lyme disease is caught early, a short course of antibiotics is typically all that's needed for a full recovery. The LymeAlert app will also help you find nearby providers and document your result for your healthcare visit.

A negative result means the specific tick tested did not carry Borrelia burgdorferi. However, ticks can carry other diseases and you could still be bitten by another tick. Monitor for symptoms and consult a doctor if you develop a rash, fever, or fatigue after any tick bite.
